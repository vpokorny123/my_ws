Bio

I'm a scientist specializing in EEG/ERP methodology and its application to psychiatric disorders. My PhD work at the University of Minnesota focused on neurophysiological indices of sensory and cognitive processing in schizophrenia — including mismatch negativity, alpha oscillations, and visual surround suppression. I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher in Vijay Mittal's lab at Northwestern University, where I focus on identifying biomarkers of psychosis risk to improve prediction and treatment. Across this work, I most enjoy turning noisy neural data into reliable, reproducible insights using R, Python, and MATLAB.
